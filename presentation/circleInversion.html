Inversion at a circle is a mirror image

Based on inversion at the unit circle in the complex plane: f(z)=1/z, where z=x+iy.

Add complex conjugation to get a true mirror image.
Put the center of inversion at w=u+iv and use a radius R for the circle. Then

f(z) = w + (R2 / |z - w |2) (z - w).

Close to the circle this makes a mirror image at its tangent.

Far away we get similar properties as for a mirror image at a straight line:
- The image of circles and lines are circles and lines.
- A circle that intersects at a right angle is mapped onto itself.
- The inside of such a circle is mapped onto itself.

The image of small region is a linear transform: Mirroring, translation, rotation and isotropic scaling.
Scaling factor (Lyapunov coefficient):

R2 / |z - w |2.

Pixels can strongly expand or shrink upon multiple circle inversion. Affects image quality.
