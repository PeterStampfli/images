Help for the single circle application

This app does its work only on your computer and your images stay on your computer. No data will be transferred.

Output image

At the left you see the output image.

Drag with the mouse or touch to change the visible part of the output image. Turn the mouse wheel, use two finger touch or type "Z" or "z" to zoom in or out.

Control panel

In the lower part of the right hand side you find a control panel.

Use it to open another input image.

You can select the view. The app opens using the direct view.

Direct view: Part of the input image is shown without distortion inside the blue circle. Outside the blue circle we see what is inside, but inverted in this circle. Note that close to the circle we have approximately a mirror image. Far away we have strong distortion.

Circle inversion view: Shows the same as the direct view, but inverted in the red circle. The center of the red circle lies on the blue circle. Inversion in the red circle maps the blue circle onto the straight blue line. Thus inversion in the blue circle becomes a mirror image at the blue line.

Save the output image as a png. You will find it in the download folder as "kaleidoscope.png"

Choose the output image size. Only the resolution changes and you will always get the same overall image.

Input image

You see it in the upper right for landscape and in the lower left for pportrait orientation. It may be partially covered by the control panel. To bring it to the foreground click or touch it. At the same time a dark disc with an arrow will appear. Click or touch remaining parts of the control panel to bring it back to the foreground.

Pixels of the input image appearing in the output image are shown in full color. Unused pixels are faded out.

Drag with the mouse or touch to shift the position of sampled pixels. With the mouse wheel or the "Z" and "z" keys you can change the size of the sampled region. With two finger touch you can zoom and rotate.

Dragging the mouse or touch on the disc with the arrow zooms and rotates the sampled region of the input image. Turning the mouse wheel or touching the "Z" and "z" keys only rotates.
